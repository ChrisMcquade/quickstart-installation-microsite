The Shift

From failed installs to guided success.

The job is simple to state and hard to do well: get a customer from an empty box to working broadband without an engineer visit. The QuickStart intent framework breaks that journey into discrete sub-intents, each mapped to grounded help content, a guided in-chat walkthrough, or a clean handoff.

Most resolve entirely within the conversation. Escalation to a human agent is reserved for the moments that genuinely need one — a suspected faulty Pod, an order-tracking query, an install that won't come good.

Grounded Answers from the source

Every factual answer is retrieved from a live crawl of virginmedia.com/help — never improvised. The agent and the self-serve website stay in lockstep.

Visual Show, don't quiz

Instead of asking a nervous customer to describe their kit, the agent shows a picture — "does yours look like this?" — to identify the Hub, the Pod, or the wall socket in one tap.

Honest Self-serve first

No suitable socket? The customer is guided to book an engineer themselves at checkout — keeping them in self-serve rather than burning a human transfer.

The Flow

One entry point. Two clean outcomes.
